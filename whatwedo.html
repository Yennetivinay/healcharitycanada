Heal Nurtures Self-Confidence Inside and Outside the Classroom. Heal has condensed India’s traditional six-day schooling into five days, so children can spend Saturdays nurturing their talents and pursuing their hobbies.

Participating in these activities fosters the children’s emotional well-being and helps develop their self-esteem. As Heal children’s self-confidence grows, by developing their skills and talents, their aspirations for escaping the poverty trap also grow. The Heal family takes great pride in the ways that Heal children showcase their many talents! Here are highlights of activities that they explore on campus:

Science Club​

We stimulate interested students’ love for science by expanding beyond the classroom. The club stimulates the students by training in the scientific method, developing problem solving, and fostering exploration and creative habits. Students participate in science projects individually and in groups, prepare for science fairs, preparecharts and models, conduct scientific experiments, among other activities. Students also read and discuss scientific literature that advances their classroom learning. By working on science projects, they become aware of career options in the scientific world

Intra House Competitions​

Students and teachers belong to one of four houses named Satya (truth), Dharma (righteousness), Nyaya (justice) and Ahimsa (non-violence). Friendly House rivalry has taught students the value of teamwork and how to be gracious in both victory and defeat. Houses participate in various competitions, including essay writing, elocution, poetry recitation, story telling, quizzes, under the support of their house teachers. The winning house enjoys the feeling of lifting the trophy at the end of the year.

Cultural Activities​

With Teachers’ Day, Children’s Day, Diwali, Sankranthi, Christmas, Independence Day, there’s always something for the children to showcase their talents. The biggest example is Heal Day, which is held every year on February 16 to mark the birthday of Heal’s founder Dr. Satya Prasad Koneru. Such events provide children with opportunities to create their own cultural programs, showcase acrobatic displays, dance, sing, and perform. Visitors marvel at the amazing talent that exists in our Heal children!

Sports Team​

Many Heal children love being a part of various Heal sports teams with the support of Heal Physical Education faculty. For example, each morning our Heal Paradise Physical Education Teacher Venkat trains 70 athletes through laps or other sports on the campus grounds. The children play traditional Indian sports such as kabbadi and kho-kho, along with track, volleyball, badminton, among others. This dedication has enabled our children to enjoy numerous sporting achievements and successes, as well the value of teamwork and staying physically healthy and active. Heal is proud of the sporting achievements of its students in its Poverty Trap program, Children’s Village, and Paradise Village. Here are some highlights from the recent school year

Innovation Club​​

The objective of Heal’s Innovation Club is to create awareness, educate, nurture and inculcate a culture of innovation amongst school children and to enable them to generate new ideas and become more innovative. We foster this innovation in a variety of ways, including encouraging the children to work on innovative projects, engaging in activities that spark creativity or workshops focused on problem solving or generating new ideas to everyday problems.

School People Leaders

We empower children to develop their leadership skills through different opportunities. Select senior students are School People Leaders (SPLs), who represent the student body. They have daily responsibilities to watch over younger students, motivate children to keep the campus clean, welcome visitors, and generally lead by example through their behavior. They also help the Principal by leading morning assemblies and giving welcome addresses. Vijaya Lakshmi, one of our past SPLs, loves the role, saying “when we were called for this role, it was an opportunity for us to practice leadership qualities which will help us later in life

Green & Food Eco Club​

We have a robust waste management system led by a student team, called the Aaga Force. The team ensures nothing is wasted at Heal Paradise. Each month, they open a tank, collect 100 kgs manure output per tank, and use the compost to organically fertilize our Heal’s gardens. They have created a revenue-generating model by selling compost bags to visitors and at exhibitions as part of its ‘Garbage to Garden’ initiative. Additionally, there are recycling and composting bins throughout the campus to instill the importance of taking care of Heal’s waste.

Math Club​ Competitions​

Heal’s Math club welcomes all students interested in mathematics. It encourages participation from those who want to expand their skills or join a competitive environment. Students engage in monthly competitions that challenge Algebra through Calculus students, all of which are suitable and appealing to students of all grades and math courses. The Math Club also instills leadership, communication, and confidence by enabling these students to tutor their peers or younger students in mathematical problems. The club has instilled a strong sense of community on the Heal campus.

Film & Photography Club​ Competitions​

Heal’s Film and Photography Club provides a supportive environment for interested students to nurture their interest in photography. It exposes children to famous photographers and cinematographers to instill an appreciation for what film and photography can communicate. Club members learn from each other and have opportunities to photograph through various school activities or photography outings. This club continues to grow in how it engages its students, with support from dedicated volunteers.

Arts Club​

The goal of Heal’s Art Club is to give students a chance to explore their own art interests. The club gives students the time to do artwork of their own choice beyond classroom art projects. A Heal volunteer, Dr. Geethika, dedicates time every week training motivated children on more advanced drawing techniques. Creating art activities help children improve their creativity, emotional development, and imaginative powe

Radio Club​

Members of Heal’s Radio Club have opportunities to harness their creativity, while building their confidence and instilling discipline. Students work together to write their own stories, songs, jokes and share on the school’s radio. The students enjoy developing their public speaking and performance skills through the Radio Club. The collection of stories, songs, and jokes are currently being consolidated in an e-book to be printed and distributed to showcase their talents.

Language Club​

The Language Club is an opportunity for students to practice English, Hindi, or Telugu beyond the classroom. Interested students learn valuable skills including evaluating their own speaking ability, preparing and giving speeches, giving impromptu talks, controlling their voice, vocabulary and gestures, debates, and more. Volunteers from this Club are also available to tutor their peers or younger students who may be having difficulty in class. The club is a great way for students with a passion for language to strengthen their skills while developing their communication skills
